I'm not that great of a writer. When I was younger I was fine with this fact because I just didn't
write that much. Why focus on a skill that I literally only used for class and no where else.
However, as I read for books and started college I realized there were levels to this writing thing.
I read articles and textbooks that were simply bad while I could clearly tell when someone knew how
to write. The good writing really helped me enjoy and understand the content better but the what
really pushed me towards improving this skill was that the bad writing was such a huge detriment to
the experience. I didn't realize just how much bad writing had been influencing what I thought about
things until I read actual good stuff. I want to get to a point where my writing doesn't make
something exiting I'm talking about feel so bad. I dream would be to write in a way that exites the
mind like some of the amazing stuff I've recently read.

To help with this I've decided to write consitently thoughout this summer in the form of blog posts
on this website. It may not be everyday but it should be at least a few per week talking about
whatever I have in mind. I'll try to have some variety in the writing where blogs will have
differnce in both tone and style which hopefully improve my writing more than just one type of a
blog. The topics themselves will most likely be all nonfiction topics surrounding things like my
daily life and some dicussion on philosophy, math, and whatever else I'm currently interesting in.
Maybe later on when I'm more confortable I'll take up some creative writing but thats for future me
to decide.

So today starts the challenge and I guess this is the first blog in this series. I've been told that
while practice is important I also need to practice correctly so I'll be trying to do some reading
on _how_ to write better as well. On top of the writing I plan to make the blogs themselves not all
text and probably add some more flavor. Of course images are a must but for many I'll probably start
incorporating block quotes/callouts to make the experience less dry. Overall this experiment should
I also serve as a great way to get better at using the tools of online blog to impact the reader.
